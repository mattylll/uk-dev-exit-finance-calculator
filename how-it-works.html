Methodology

How the calculator works

The cost model

Dev exit finance cost is the sum of four components:

  • Monthly interest — facility × monthly rate, paid each month (serviced) or accrued to exit (rolled)
  • Arrangement fee — facility × fee %, charged at drawdown, typically 1–2%
  • Exit fee — facility × fee %, charged at repayment; increasingly rare on dev exit product
  • Legal + valuation — fixed £3–15k range depending on complexity; not included in the calculator because it varies so much

Serviced vs rolled

This calculator uses the serviced model — interest paid monthly, no compounding. This matches how most UK developers actually structure dev exit facilities once sales start generating cash. Formula: interest = facility × rate × months.

For a rolled-up model, multiply facility by (1 + rate)^months − 1. On a 9-month, 0.7% pm facility the rolled cost is about 3% higher than serviced — the difference grows non-linearly with time.

The refinance comparison

Some sales periods run longer than expected and a term refinance would be cheaper than running dev exit indefinitely. The comparison uses:

refi cost = facility × annual rate × (months / 12) + fixed costs

Fixed costs default to £45k (arrangement + legals on a typical £3m commercial term mortgage). Below 6 months, dev exit usually wins; above 12 months, refi usually does.

Assumptions baked into the calculator

  • Monthly interest is flat over the term (no step-down if units sell early)
  • Arrangement fee is deducted from the facility at drawdown
  • No break cost if you exit early (most UK dev exit products have no ERC)
  • Refi cost is flat — no early repayment on the refi itself

What the calculator doesn't model

Partial drawdowns, amortisation as units sell, interest-rate step-ups on extension, penalty interest if you breach sales covenants, and stamp duty on any refinance. These can meaningfully change the answer on edge cases. For a deal-specific model, consult a broker or cash flow advisor.
